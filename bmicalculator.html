⚖️ What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value derived from a person's weight and height. It is a widely used screening tool to categorize whether a person's body weight is within a healthy range for their height.

The formula is: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m)

📊 BMI Categories

The World Health Organization defines four standard weight categories:

UnderweightBelow 18.5
Normal Weight18.5 – 24.9
Overweight25.0 – 29.9
Obese30.0 and above

💡 Why does it matter?

BMI helps identify potential health risks. Individuals with a BMI outside the normal range may face increased risks of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, hypertension, and other conditions. It's a quick first step in a health assessment.

⚠️ Limitations

BMI is a general indicator and does not account for muscle mass, bone density, age, or fat distribution. Athletes may have a high BMI without excess fat. Always consult a healthcare professional for a complete health evaluation.
